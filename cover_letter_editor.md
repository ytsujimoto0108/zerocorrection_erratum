# Cover Letter for Erratum Submission

**To:**
The Editor-in-Chief,
*Research Synthesis Methods*

**Subject:** Request for Publication of an Erratum regarding our article "The impact of continuity correction methods in Cochrane reviews with single-zero trials with rare events: A meta-epidemiological study"

Dear Editor,

I am writing to you regarding our recent article titled "**The impact of continuity correction methods in Cochrane reviews with single-zero trials with rare events: A meta-epidemiological study**" (Tsujimoto Y, Tsutsumi Y, Kataoka Y, Shiroshita A, Efthimiou O, Furukawa TA), which was published in *Research Synthesis Methods* in 2024.

We recently discovered that a portion of our eligible data was inadvertently omitted from the original analyses. This issue was caused by a random technical error that occurred during the data downloading phase via our scraping script. Specifically, out of the complete sample, only 885 meta-analyses were successfully downloaded and included in the initially published report. We sincerely apologize for this oversight.

Upon discovering this error, we obtained the complete, correct dataset consisting of 2,783 meta-analyses and re-ran all of our original statistical analyses.

Importantly, after thoroughly recalculating the data, we found that the results were not significantly affected, and none of our overarching conclusions have changed. The central finding—that evaluating single-zero studies with the Mantel-Haenszel model with a fixed continuity correction yields results substantially different from alternative, more appropriate models without correction—remains remarkably robust with the complete data representation.

Given the importance of accuracy and transparency in the scientific record, we have prepared a formal Erratum to correct the sample sizes, adjust the descriptive tables (Table 1 and Table 2), and provide the updated version of Figure 2. Furthermore, we have uploaded the complete re-extracted dataset and the R analytical scripts to a public GitHub repository (https://github.com/ytsujimoto0108/zerocorrection_erratum) to guarantee full reproducibility.

We respectfully request that this Erratum be considered for publication to update the literature and clarify the data size for the readers. The text of the Erratum and the updated tables and figures are attached for your review.

Please let us know if there is any further information or documentation you require from our end. We deeply appreciate your time, understanding, and assistance in correcting this matter.

Sincerely,
